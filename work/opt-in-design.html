Insurance Digital Download

Commonwealth Bank of Australia

April, 2019

Overview

For business and customer outcomes, we needed to redesign part of the Car and Home Insurance sales flows, allowing customers to opt in for receiving their policy documents by email.

What I did

I designed a responsive web experience that:

  • Allows customers to opt in for receiving their documents digitally
  • Updated CommBank's web pattern for mobile input and validation
  • Used the default cognitive bias to encourage take-up and reduce business costs

My thought process

Users: Existing and new customers of CommBank who are digitally active and looking to open an insurance policy for their home, contents or car.

Problem Space: Opting in to receive policy documents had both customer and business benefits, but a range of architectural, system and customer problems needed to be catered for to achieve a seamless experience.

Goals: Update the existing sales flows with a seamless opt in experience and encourage take-up.

Process: Bushfires and wildfires are often unpredictable, fast moving and poorly understood. People underestimate how long a fire takes to reach them and once it does, it is difficult to tell which direction it's spreading in. This is life threatening and has lead to people's deaths.

Design Rationale

Archtectural and System Constraints: The reality of working across different databases and legacy systems meant that the first stage of the experience was designed with the following unavoidable constraints:

Translating Complexity: One of my driving principles throughout the project was to take the system and business requirement complexity and ensure that this was not experienced by customers. At the end of the day, customers just want an insurance policy and to receive their documents by email. This shouldn't be difficult.

Opt in Design with Cognitive Bias: The first question in the experience was phrased intentionally, defaulting customers to receiving their documents by email. Customers are able to opt-out by clicking the secondary link which was also intentionally phrased as "post" instead of "mail" to avoid confusion.

Process Transparency: If customers decide to opt in, there were two possible scenarios:

  • They are an existing customer and we have their contact details. Meaning the design needed to confirm the details and communicate expectations.
  • Adding visual indicators for users who have seen the fire provides users with an aid in estimating where the fire is and how serious it is
  • Adding wind direction allows users to make an informed decision about which direction to flee the fire
