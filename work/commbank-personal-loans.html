Personal Loans (In Progress)

Putting customer outcomes first in personal loan applications.

CommBank - Full project details coming soon.

Context

Changes in regulation meant that CommBank was required to use Credit Bureau data to cross-check the debts customers declare when applying for credit (e.g. loans and credit cards). This presented a design opportunity to meet regulatory requirements, improve customers' financial wellbeing outcomes and update the aesthetics our personal loans origination form.


Problem Space

Customer problem
Currently, the bank would use information that the customer disclosed in their application and their transaction data. However, customers might potentially miss or misreport debts held with other institutions. This meant two things were happening:

  • Customers who shouldn’t be eligible for loans were getting loans and risking their financial wellbeing
  • Customers who would otherwise be eligible were being declined unnecessarily

Design challenges
The process of matching external debt information, customer declared information and their transactional data is complex. A "full proof solution" would costly and unfeasible. Therefore, the designs had to accomodate for the following constraints:

  • Even if a customer had declared a debt, the data we receive might be out of date (e.g. out of date home loan repayment amount because of interest rate changes)
  • Customers assume that the bank will have a complete and accurate record of their debts.
